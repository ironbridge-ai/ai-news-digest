OpenAI Blog

GPT-5.6 Sol Raises the Bar for Coding, Science, and Cybersecurity

GPT-5.6 Sol is OpenAI's next-generation model preview, positioned around stronger performance in coding, scientific reasoning, and cybersecurity. The important signal is not just that the model can answer harder questions. It is that OpenAI is pairing those capabilities with its most advanced safety stack, which shows how seriously the industry now treats powerful models as business-critical infrastructure.

This unlocks a bigger client conversation about IT strategy. SMEs should not ask only, "Which AI tool should we buy?" They should ask, "Which parts of our business could be accelerated if expert-level coding, analysis, and cyber support were available on demand?" For RAMSAC, this is a natural opening for AI readiness reviews, secure rollout planning, and conversations about where AI belongs inside managed IT and cybersecurity operations.

Google DeepMind

Gemini 3.5 Flash Learns to Use Computers, Not Just Chat About Them

Google DeepMind introduced computer use in Gemini 3.5 Flash, a step beyond the traditional chatbot experience. Instead of simply producing text, the model can interact with software environments in a more practical way. This is the direction that turns an AI agent from a helpful adviser into something closer to a digital team member that can carry out a process.

This unlocks a powerful SME conversation about workflow automation. If AI can use the same tools staff already use, then routine admin, reporting, inbox triage, form completion, and system updates become candidates for automation. For RAMSAC, the commercial angle is clear: businesses will need managed IT controls, access permissions, audit trails, and Microsoft 365 governance before they let AI act inside their systems.

OpenAI Blog

GPT-5 Helps Solve a Three-Year Immunology Mystery

OpenAI reported that GPT-5 Pro helped immunologist Derya Unutmaz solve a long-running research mystery about T cells. The breakthrough matters because the model was not just summarising papers. It was helping connect evidence, reason through possible explanations, and offer useful scientific insight in a complex field.

This gives RMs a memorable way to explain AI's value to business leaders: AI is becoming a thinking partner, not just a writing assistant. Most SMEs are not solving immunology problems, but they do have trapped expertise in documents, emails, spreadsheets, CRM notes, and senior employees' heads. RAMSAC can help clients turn that knowledge into usable business advantage through Microsoft 365 optimisation, secure data foundations, and practical AI strategy.

OpenAI Blog

OpenAI Launches Daybreak to Help Patch Vulnerabilities at Scale

OpenAI introduced Daybreak as a set of tools for securing organisations, with a focus on vulnerability discovery and repair. The announcement includes Codex Security and GPT-5.5-Cyber, plus Patch the Planet, an initiative to support maintainers who keep widely used software projects secure. The big shift is that AI is moving into the full security workflow, from spotting a weakness to helping produce a fix.

This directly strengthens the RAMSAC cybersecurity story. SMEs often know patching matters, but struggle with speed, prioritisation, and capacity. AI-assisted security does not remove the need for expert oversight. It makes expert teams more productive, which is exactly the promise RAMSAC can take to clients: better monitoring, faster response, and smarter prioritisation without asking the client to build a large internal security team.

Ars Technica AI

Claude Cloning Case Shows Why AI Governance Now Matters

Anthropic alleges that Alibaba mined Claude through millions of interactions in an attempt to copy model behaviour. The AI-specific issue is model distillation, where one AI system is trained by studying the answers of another. This matters because the outputs of advanced models are becoming intellectual property, not just chat responses.

The client message is calm and practical. This does not weaken the case for AI adoption. It strengthens the case for proper AI governance, supplier due diligence, logging, access control, and Data isolation when businesses use AI with sensitive information. For RAMSAC, the takeaway is simple: clients need a trusted partner to help them use AI confidently without losing control of their data, workflows, or competitive knowledge.
